We started the TecfaMOO around November 1994. At that time it wasn't a real project, but something like "let's have MOO and see what we can do". A few ideas have come up since then, i.e. see the short information on TecfaMOO (including a list research questions). Some of those ideas have turned into more serious projects or at least "usages".

1. Spreading the News ....

(Documentation, Teaching, and Technology Transfer)

Documentation on MOOs and MOOing

Documentation on MOOs and MOOing is sometimes hard find and certain things are missing. Therefore we decided to spend some effort on that.
  • The evolving TecfaMOO book - part I This is an unfinished attempt to build a conceptual MOO resource book.
  • The evolving TecfaMOO book - part II This is an ongoing attempt to build a MOO builder's and programmer's manual.
  • Also, we decided to created educational MUD/text-base VR resource pages and host other information useful for the community. See the MOO Comunity page for details.

MOO Education at TECFA

  • Our Diploma Students are introduced to text-based VR (MOOs) in 2 courses: Staf14 (Introduction to CMC and information systems) and staf-18 (introduction to virtual environments) See the MOO Education at Tecfa page for details.
  • Since Fall 96, introduction to programming is taught with the MOO (see Staf14 and the tutorial section in the TecfaMOO Manuel
  • TecfaMOO Student Projects: Some student work is associated with bigger research projects (e.g. bootnap). Also some are using the MOO for a master thesis or a practical work requirement (stage). [information on those will appear sometimes]

    Documentation developed for our courses may be of use to others: See the Builder's and Programmer's Informationpage.

4. MOO as CMC Tool for teaching and cooperation

  • TecfaMOO is increasingly used as non-intrusive CMC tool for internal communication
  • Since our postgraduate "STAF"diploma in educational technology is organized as "semi-distance curriculum", the MOO is being also increasingly used to communicate with our students at remote sites.
  • We are hosting virtual sessions of researchers (e.g. people from the ESF/LHM programme). See the The VDML/MOO report for more details or "Virtual Environments for Education, Research and Life" a position paper.
  • The international Pangea project brings together kids from 3 continents. A short text in french is available as well as un "rapport de stage.
Some of these uses of TecfaMOO might be turned into research projects.
